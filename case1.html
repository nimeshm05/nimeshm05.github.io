The scheduler app

Conceptualize a mobile app experience for students to make appointments with stakeholders of the campus.

Project Type

Team project with University Innovation Fellows
of the college.

My Role

I was involved in conducting user interviews and surveys, and was responsible for design and prototype.

Duration

The design section of project was completed in 8 months and the development of the app is under progress.

Skills experienced

User journey mapping, prototyping user interfaces with Abode illustrator and Adobe XD respectively, stakeholder interviews.

Project Overview

This project is designed for students to meliorate their experience of scheduling a meet with professors to improve mutual schedule convenience. The project is in implementation phase and UX research is conducted with students and other stakeholders of the campus

Observation

From the past 6 months, I’ve observed that the student members from various student organizations and clubs in the college often get frustrated for being impuissant. By conducting a few interviews and tea-table talks with the students, I observed and recognized one common problem, i.e, differing schedules between students and professors(or other stakeholders). Communication between different stakeholders of the campus is very important for the comprehensive growth of innovation in the campus.

Interviews and Synthesis

After observations, the team conducted chronological interviews over the past 8 months with the students on the campus. By interacting with them, we observed their behavioral responses and discerned their concerns. We analyzed various students' responses and developed an inference. Another interesting fact is, we didn’t conduct surveys through Google forms because the team wanted to empathize and understand their concerns deeply, and hence the interview process lasted for a few months(1-2). We structured the synthesis in the following way(Below mentioned is just one example):

The synthesis

  • I talked to XXXX who is a freshman at VVCE.
  • XXXX is an eloquent person and is amazed to see various extra-curricular clubs in college
  • XXXX felt infuriated for not being able to meet professors whenever he wanted to meet them.
  • I wonder if this means that students like XXXX cast down if they lose their precious time waiting to meet professors.
  • How might we help students connect with professors at a scheduled time with mutual convenience?

The Problem

Students in the campus communicate and coordinate with different stakeholders for numerous reasons. For example, student leaders communicate with faculty coordinators to acquire consent to organize club related activities. Students often have to walk around the huge campus and wait for hours until the respective stakeholder is free or, is present in his/her office. Students lose their valuable time and delay the work. This results in the development of disinterest over some time and students eventually lack the motivation to conduct club related activities.

The Challenge

How might we help the students engage their valuable time with different stakeholders of the campus? The students and other stakeholders of the campus have different time schedules each day. Hence, the challenge rested in identifying a mutually convenient time and acquaint the students with time schedule information of different stakeholders of the campus.

Major Goals

After conducting interviews, studying student and stakeholder behaviors, identifying the challenges and problems, the team ideated on innovative solutions. One such optimal and innovative solution is to develop a mobile app that displays the time schedule information of all the stakeholders of the campus. By building the mobile app, we seek help from the student tech clubs and therefore leveraging opportunities for the Tech students to work on a project. The integrated work between various students leveraged a lot of opportunities and solves the problem too.

Wireframes and Prototypes

I was responsible to create wireframes, user flows, design, and prototypes of the mobile app. This was an intriguing piece of work for me.

For design and prototypes, I used Adobe XD and Adobe Illustrator software tools. Although Adobe XD provided issues with font variations, I was able to convince and coordinate the developers by communicating with them.

Login page

This conceptualization has two login page processes, one for the phone number and another for USN(College ID for each student). The reason for the two login page process is to make the user flow smoothly instead of spending more time to fill information on one interface.

Landing page

The landing page displays relevant information for the user. The students can use the search bar to search for the name of the faculty to check availability time. The landing page also displays the upcoming activities that allow the students to quickly access information about activity and faculty associated with that activity. This design is minimal for a landing page and hence more desirable for the users.

Calendar Page

The calendar page displays the availability time of professors. The timetable for each professor has been stored in the backend and accordingly, the app calendar updates with changes made to the professor's schedule. The green boxes indicate that the professor is available during those hours.

Research and Iteration

So far, the only first phase of prototypes is made. The responses received after deploying the app can help us analyze student behavior, based on which we can modify or add new elements to the wide spectrum of characteristics.

Prototypes were tested with the students and 80% of them admired the idea of deploying an app mainly for scheduling appointments with professors, while the rest 20% wanted an overall integration of all the college-related information(Attendance status, Fee payment status, Club activity notifications) in one app.

Conclusion and Learning

From the above project, I understood that problems can be viewed as challenges, challenges can be viewed as opportunities and, opportunities are the pathway to unseen optimal possibilities.

By keeping the users(students and professors) at the forefront, I could derive insights. This enabled me to empathize with them and therefore found the motivation to solve problems. Deploying design thinking principles for this particular project aided me to analyze and understand “Design thinking for higher education”.

The crazy catch: Even I was suffering from the same problem at times. :)
